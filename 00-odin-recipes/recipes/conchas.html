Pan Dulce

Description

Conchas are a beloved Mexican pastry, recognized for their signature shell-like design etched into a sweet, crumbly topping. These soft, fluffy sweet breads are made from a lightly sweetened dough, typically enriched with butter and eggs, and topped with a sugary crust.A staple of Mexican bakeries, conchas are often enjoyed with coffee or hot chocolate as a comforting breakfast or snack.

with a prep time of 25 minutes, cook time of 20 minutes, for 12 servings sure to be a crowd favorite!

Ingredients

  • 3 tablespoons warm wate
  • 2 1/2 teaspoons active dry yeast
  • 1 cup melted unslated butter
  • 1 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 cup white granulated sugar
  • 3/4 cup evaporated milk
  • 2 teaspoons kosher salt
  • 3 teaspoons pure vanilla extract
  • 2 teaspoons ground cinnamon
  • 2 large eggs, at room temperature
  • 5 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons cocoa powder
  • food coloring of choice

Steps

  1. To the bowl of a stand-up mixer with the hook attachment add the warm water. Pour the active dry yeast on top and mix in into the water. Allow to stand until foamy, about 5 to 7 minutes.
  2. When the active dry yeast is foamy, pour in the vegetable oil, melted butter, granulated sugar, evaporated milk, salt, vanilla, ground cinnamon and eggs. Break up the eggs with the back of a spatula and allow the dough hook to mix it up a bit, about 30 seconds
  3. pour the flour in all at once and run the machine for about 5 minutes on medium speed, until the dough is soft and smooth. If you’re doing this by hand, once the dough comes together, you'll want to dump it out onto your floured counter and knead it for about 10 minutes
  4. Rub a large bowl with oil or spray it with cooking spray and place the dough in the center of the bowl. Cover it with a clean kitchen towel and allow it to rise for about 1 hour, until doubled in size.
  5. for streusell topping, In the bowl of stand-up mixer with the paddle attachment add the butter, sugar, flour and vanilla extract. Mix until smooth
  6. Divide the dough in two. To one part of the topping, add a drop of food coloring gel. To the other topping, add the cocoa powder and knead until it’s cohesive.
  7. Once the dough has risen, divide the dough into 12 equal parts. Or better yet, weigh them into balls of 100g. Form the balls of dough into rounds
  8. Take about 2 tablespoons of the topping and rollout using our palms, flatting it into a thin round. Drape it over the round of dough, patting down lightly. Using a knife, cut grooves in the topping like a clam shell.
  9. Cover and let rise until nearly doubled, about 30 minutes.
  10. Preheat oven to 350 degrees. Bake for about 18 to 20 minutes, or until lightly golden brown.
